Heatsink

What?

  • In 2022 the car failed due to the motor overheating so in 2023 a heatsink was added to the motor casing

How?

  • I made two parametric heatsink models; one with annular fins and one with radial fins
  • I then set up a thermal simulation to ensure the motor would not overheat
  • Another engineer then used the simulations to adjust the parameters and optimise the design

Results

  • The motor still overheated due to a lack of air ducts in the car's aero shell and a poorly chosen gear ratio. The heatsink will be used again this year as it was not the reason for failure

Other Parts

What?

  • I designed the brake light detection system and the car's control panel
  • I manufactured various mounting and wheel parts

How?

  • I used Fusion 360 to design and car's control panel and brake light holder
  • The mounting blocks and wheel parts were machined out of aluminium on a lathe and mill

Results

  • The brake light detection system was triggered as soon as the brake leave was pulled and passed scrutineering
  • The control panel held the horn, speed control, and go button with a bolted lid to hold them securely in place
  • The machined blocks held the motor in place. The wheels were one of the best-built parts of the car and we will be using them again this year
  • My contributions to the car last year have earned me the role of electrical technical lead this year
