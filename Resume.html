Experience

SUMMER 2023 ELECTRICAL, CONTROL AND INSTRUMENTATION INTERN, FRAZER-NASH CONSULTANCY
I spent 13 weeks working in the Bristol EC&I North team at Frazer-Nash. Over this time, I worked on a variety of projects which involved PowerFactory modelling to look at the grid impact of a nuclear fusion power station, researching and building electrical and thermal models using Simscape, designing the electrical schematic for a submarine test rig, drawing single line diagrams for a nuclear power station, writing supporting design documentation for a nuclear power station and evaluating different PLC options.

SEPTEMBER 2022 – PRESENT DAY, BRISTOL RACING
In my free time have worked in the Electrical and Powertrain team for Bristol Racing for just over a year. My work includes characterisation of the car's battery, designing a parameterised heatsink model and optimising its design through thermal simulations, implementation of the brake light detection circuit, and design of the driver’s control holder. I am also mill and lathe trained which I have used to make many of the metal parts required for the car. My work earned me the position of electrical technical co-lead on this year’s car.

SUMMER 2021 AND 2022, RF ENGINEERING INTERN, SPIRIT SEMICONDUCTOR
I have completed two 9-week summer internships at Spirit Semiconductor, a start-up designing RF electronics for 5G. Over this time, I completed tasks including:

  • Designing a PCB which interfaced EVBs to a 2kV power supply.
  • Making a diplexer to meet specifications by running EM simulations in microwave office, HFFS, and ADS. I tested different technologies to produce a design over 10 times cheaper than buying a premade diplexer.
  • Figuring out and documenting how to use an old curve tracer for characterising ESD protection structures on RF ICs.
  • Using network analysers, signal generators, RF IC test setups, power tools, and soldering.
  • Laying out and assembling a 0.5m x 1m test setup to split a signal between thirteen power amplifiers.

OCTOBER 2019 – MARCH 2020, ENGINEERING EDUCATION SCHEME
I was one of six students challenged to build a lifting device to remove up to 500Kg of radioactive waste from a particle accelerator. I contributed the core idea used in our final design. I also sized the geometry of the design by doing stress calculations. Finally, I oversaw the write-up. The project was extremely well received, earning me the Gold Industrial Cadet award.
